Lesson Plan - Unit 12 (5 lectures + test)

Schedule: Sunday – Thursday (1 hour each). Test: Next Sunday (1 hour). No classes on Friday/Saturday.

Digital tools: Quizlet sets (vocabulary), Kahoot (review games), Wordwall (interactive activities).

DayDictation WordsWarm-up / Guided PracticeIndependent Practice (Digital & Pair)Wrap-up & HW
Sunday head, eye, ear, nose, mouth, neck Body parts vocabulary with Quizlet flashcards. Teacher points to body parts: “This is my head.” Wordwall matching (body part ↔ picture). Students use Quizlet “Learn”. Pair work: “What is this? – It’s your nose.” Kahoot body parts quiz. HW: Label a drawing of a person with 10 body parts.
Monday arm, hand, leg, foot, back, stomach More body parts with Quizlet. Teacher asks “What do you use to walk? – Legs and feet.” Wordwall “body parts” drag & drop. Students play “Simon says” to practice. Kahoot body parts (advanced). HW: Write 5 sentences “I have two arms.”
Tuesday headache, stomachache, toothache, earache, cold, cough Learn common illnesses using Quizlet. Students repeat and make sentences "I have a headache." Wordwall “symptom → problem” match. Pair work: “What’s the matter? – I have a cold.” Kahoot health problems. HW: Write 5 sentences using “I have a/an ...”.
Wednesday fever, flu, sore throat, rest, medicine, doctor Learn more symptoms and advice. Teacher introduces 'take medicine' and 'see a doctor'. Wordwall “advice” match. Students give advice: “If you have a fever, take medicine.” Kahoot imperatives (advice). HW: Write 5 pieces of advice for common illnesses.
Thursday Review all body parts, illnesses, advice Review with Kahoot (full unit). Teacher leads conversation “I don’t feel well” (p.??). Quizlet “Test” mode. Role‑play: doctor and patient – “What’s the matter? – I have a headache.” Final revision. HW: Prepare for Sunday’s test (body parts, have/feel, imperatives).
Friday No class (official weekend)
Saturday No class (official weekend)
Next Sunday FINAL TEST – Unit 12 (1 hour)
Dictation (10 words: body parts/illnesses), fill in blanks (have/feel, imperatives), matching (symptom ↔ advice), short writing (describe a health problem and give advice), oral (ask & answer “What’s the matter?”).

Weak performance plan: Extra Quizlet practice, peer tutoring, audio recordings, Class Dojo rewards.
